10 Breathtaking Drone Shots Every Wedding Needs

PanoptesDrones Teamâ€¢Feb 5, 2026â€¢9 min read

Weddings are one of the most emotionally charged events in anyone's life, and in Lagos, they're celebrated with a grandeur and vibrancy that few cities can match. From elegant beach ceremonies at Elegushi to lavish receptions in Victoria Island's finest venues, Lagos weddings are spectacles worth capturing from every possible angle â€” including from the sky.

Drone footage has become the defining element that separates standard wedding videos from truly cinematic productions. Here are the 10 essential drone shots that every wedding video should include, along with specific tips for executing them in Lagos locations.

1. The Venue Reveal

Start your footage with a dramatic wide shot that slowly reveals the entire wedding venue. The drone begins high and distant, then sweeps in toward the decorated venue, giving viewers a sense of scale and grandeur. For beach weddings at Elegushi or Tarkwa Bay, start over the ocean and fly inland to reveal the setup. For indoor venues like Balmoral Convention Centre, orbit the exterior at golden hour to capture the building and arriving guests.

This shot works best when the venue is fully decorated but before guests arrive â€” the empty, pristine setup creates a "magazine shoot" aesthetic that couples adore.

2. The Processional Tracking Shot

Follow the bridal procession from above as it moves from the church to the reception. In Lagos, this often means capturing the convoy along iconic routes like the Lekki-Ikoyi Link Bridge or Third Mainland Bridge. The drone flies parallel to the vehicles at a consistent altitude, creating a dynamic, movie-like sequence that establishes the journey between venues.

This shot requires careful coordination with the driver and a drone with long battery life, as the distance can be significant depending on the route.

3. The Overhead Dance Floor

Position the drone directly above the dance floor during the reception's most energetic moments. From this bird's-eye perspective, the swirl of colourful aso-ebi outfits, the choreagraphed dance routines, and the circular crowd formations create mesmerising, kaleidoscopic patterns. This shot is uniquely aerial â€” no ground-level camera can replicate it.

Use a slow shutter speed (1/30 to 1/60) for the overhead dance floor shot to create a subtle motion blur that conveys energy and movement. Keep the drone at a safe distance â€” at least 10 metres above the crowd â€” and ensure obstacle avoidance is enabled.

4. The Couple's Portrait Orbit

When the couple steps away for portrait photos, execute a slow, gentle orbit around them. The drone circles at a 30-45 degree downward angle, keeping the couple centred while the background rotates beautifully. For Lagos weddings, position the couple near water (lagoon, pool, or beach) or against a striking architectural backdrop for maximum effect.

This 15-30 second shot often becomes the hero moment of the highlight reel â€” the one clip that the couple shares repeatedly on Instagram.

5. The Guest Arrival Overview

Capture a time-compressed aerial view of guests arriving. The drone hovers at a fixed position above the entrance, showing the flow of beautifully dressed guests walking toward the venue. At Nigerian weddings, the variety of outfits, colours, and accessories makes this shot particularly vibrant and engaging.

6. The Sunset Silhouette

If the wedding schedule allows, position the drone behind the couple at sunset so they're silhouetted against the glowing sky. This works spectacularly on Lagos beaches, where the Atlantic sunset provides a palette of oranges, pinks, and purples. The drone should be low â€” just 3-5 metres above the couple â€” to capture the wide horizon with them as small, intimate figures against the vast sky.

7. The Table Setup Sweep

Before guests sit down for the reception, fly the drone slowly over the table arrangements. The overhead perspective reveals the full table layout, floral arrangements, place settings, and decorative details that hours of effort have produced. Wedding planners particularly love this shot as portfolio material.

8. The Crowd Reaction Pull-Back

During key moments â€” the first kiss, the first dance, the cake cutting â€” position the drone close to the crowd, then rapidly pull back and rise to reveal the entire guest assembly reacting. This "pull-back" creates a dramatic scale shift from intimate to epic that amplifies the emotion of the moment.

9. The Exit Send-Off

As the couple departs the venue at the end of the night, capture their exit from above. Whether they're leaving in a decorated car, walking through a sparkler send-off, or being showered with confetti, the aerial perspective adds a fairy-tale quality to the closing moments. Follow the departing vehicle briefly as it drives away for a cinematic ending.

10. The Full Grounds Establishing Shot

At the highest comfortable altitude, capture a wide shot that shows the entire venue, parking, gardens, and surrounding area. This establishing shot is usually placed at the beginning of the highlight reel to set the scene, or used as a transition between ceremony and reception footage. It gives viewers the "big picture" context that makes all the close-up shots more meaningful.

A wedding happens once, but great aerial footage makes the memories eternal. The couples who invest in drone coverage consistently say it was the best decision of their wedding planning â€” the footage captures the scale, energy, and emotion in ways that ground cameras simply cannot.

Planning Your Wedding Drone Coverage

Timing Is Everything

Coordinate closely with the wedding planner and photographer to identify the exact moments when drone coverage will be most impactful. Create a shot list in advance and time each flight window so the drone is in the air during key moments rather than hovering needlessly.

Respect the Ceremony

Drones can be noisy. During quiet, intimate moments â€” especially during vows and prayers â€” keep the drone high enough that the buzzing doesn't distract. Or better yet, get the venue establishing shots before the ceremony starts and save the closer footage for the reception when music and conversation mask the drone sound.

Weather Contingency

Lagos weather is unpredictable, especially during rainy season (April to October). Have a backup plan for drone coverage. If outdoor flight isn't possible, focus on interior shots for critical moments and plan to capture outdoor aerial footage during any weather breaks.

Hire a Professional Pilot

Wedding drone coverage is not the time for amateur flying. An experienced pilot can execute complex shots safely while the photographer focuses on ground coverage.
